## Start here: what to do first

The first step in the SNAP application process is gathering the necessary documentation. This step often trips up applicants, but being prepared can make all the difference. Here's what you need to do:

- **Gather Income Documents**: Collect recent pay stubs, tax returns, or other income proofs. This helps verify your financial situation.
- **Identify Household Members**: List everyone living in your household, as this affects your benefit amount.
- **Prepare Housing and Utility Bills**: These documents are needed to calculate your expense-related deductions.

## FAQs

### What documents do I need for SNAP application?
You'll need proof of identity, income, and expenses. This includes pay stubs, utility bills, and identification.

### How long does the SNAP application process take?
Generally, it takes about 30 days from application to receiving benefits, but this can vary by state.

### Can I apply for SNAP if I have a job?
Yes, having a job doesn't disqualify you. Eligibility is based on income and household size.

### What happens if I miss my SNAP interview?
You should contact your local SNAP office immediately to reschedule. Missing the interview can delay your application.
