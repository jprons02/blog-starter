## Start here: what to do first

The first step in dealing with tax debt is not to panic. The IRS offers several options to help you manage your obligations:

- **Determine what you owe**: Ensure you have an accurate understanding of your total tax liability.
- **Contact the IRS**: If you believe you'll have trouble paying, reach out to the IRS as soon as possible.
- **Consider your payment options**: Review available IRS payment plans and decide which one fits your situation best.

## Short-Term Payment Plans: A Quick Fix for Temporary Issues

For those experiencing short-term financial difficulties, the IRS offers a short-term payment plan. Here's what you need to know:

- **Eligibility**: Generally available if you owe less than $100,000 in combined tax, penalties, and interest.
- **Duration**: Allows up to 180 days to pay the full amount.
- **Fees**: No set-up fee, but penalties and interest continue to accrue until the balance is paid.

Imagine you received an unexpected medical bill that temporarily impacts your ability to pay taxes. A short-term payment plan can provide breathing room while you manage both obligations.

- **Apply online**: Easily accessible through the IRS website.
- **No need for extensive paperwork**: Typically requires only basic financial information.
- **Flexibility**: Adjust payments if your financial situation changes.

## Installment Agreements: Spreading Out Your Payments

Installment agreements let you pay off your tax debt over time, making it more manageable for many taxpayers:

- **Longer repayment period**: These plans extend payments up to 72 months.
- **Eligibility**: Suitable if you owe $50,000 or less in combined taxes, penalties, and interest.
- **Setup fee**: A fee applies, but it can be reduced if you agree to automatic withdrawals.

Consider a scenario where you're balancing monthly expenses on a limited income. An installment agreement lets you spread out your tax payments, reducing the immediate financial burden.

- **Set up online or by phone**: Multiple ways to apply, depending on your preference.
- **Avoid collection actions**: Keeps the IRS from taking enforcement actions while you're making payments.
- **Modify if needed**: You can request modifications if your circumstances change.

## FAQs

### What is the deadline to apply for an IRS payment plan?
You can apply at any time, but it's best to do so as soon as you realize you can't pay your taxes on time.

### Can I change my payment plan once it's set up?
Yes, you can request changes to your payment plan if your financial situation changes.

### Are there penalties for paying late even with a payment plan?
Yes, interest and penalties continue to accrue until your balance is fully paid.

### What happens if I miss a payment on my installment plan?
Missing a payment could lead to defaulting on the agreement, which may result in the IRS taking enforcement actions.
